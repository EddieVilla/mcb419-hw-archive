  1. When the bot moves in a straght line it sweeps out a rectangular path with a width equal to the bot diameter and a length equal to the distance traveled (velocity times time). Using the density of pellets in the arena, compute the average energy collected per unit time for such a path, ignoring the effects of boundaries.
    pellet density = 100 pellets/(400 px * 400 px) = 0.000625 pellets / px2
    bot area covered per tick = diameter * speed = 40 px * (3 px/tick) = 120 px2/tick
    expected pellets per tick = 120*100/(400*400) = 0.075 pellets/tick
  2. Based on your analysis, approximately what value of wanderNoise appears to be optimal?

  3. How does the measured foraging efficiency at the optimal noise level compare to the theoretical value calculated in question 1? Why are these values different?
    measured around 0.045 pellets per tick; this is lower than theoretical value of 0.075 due to inefficiencies associated with boundaries and trajectory overlap
  4. Why does foraging efficiency decrease at lower values of wanderNoise relative to the optimum?
    at low wanderNoise, the bot spends more time stuck at the boundaries
  5. Why does foraging efficiency decrease at higher values of wanderNoise relative to the optimum?
    at high wanderNoise, the bot trajectory is compact and overlaps areas that have already been explored
